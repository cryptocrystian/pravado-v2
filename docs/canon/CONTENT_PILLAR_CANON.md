## 4. Pravado Differentiation (Why Users Switch)

This is where the pillar becomes best-in-class, not just competitive.

### 4.1 Content Is Treated as Authority Infrastructure

Other tools ask:
> "Is this content optimized?"

Pravado asks:
> "Does this content increase AI-readable authority?"

Every piece of content is evaluated as:
- An entity reinforcement
- A citation opportunity
- An AI ingestion signal

**This is the fundamental shift.**

### 4.2 CiteMind Governance (Non-Optional)

The Content Pillar operates inside CiteMind constraints.

That means:
- Claims must be verifiable
- Assertions must be entity-grounded
- Sources must be citation-worthy
- Repetition must be intentional, not spammy

> CiteMind does not write content.
> CiteMind qualifies content.

This prevents:
- AI hallucination risk
- Over-optimization
- Trust dilution

### 4.3 Content Is Multi-Surface by Default

Content is never "just a blog post."

Each asset has:
- **Primary format** (e.g., long-form article)
- **Secondary derivatives:**
  - PR pitch excerpts
  - AEO snippets
  - AI-ready summaries
  - Social fragments
- **Cross-pillar hooks** (PR, AEO, Command Center)

**This is structural reuse, not manual repurposing.**
