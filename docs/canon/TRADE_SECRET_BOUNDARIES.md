# TRADE SECRET BOUNDARIES

> **Status:** CANONICAL
> **Authority:** This document defines information classification and disclosure boundaries.
> **Classification:** INTERNAL — Do not disclose outside Pravado
> **Last Updated:** 2026-01-13

---

## 1. Purpose

This document establishes clear boundaries for what information about Pravado's technology and operations may be disclosed, to whom, and under what circumstances. Proper classification protects defensible IP while enabling effective sales, marketing, and customer success.

---

## 2. Classification Levels

### 2.1 Level Definitions

| Level | Definition | Audience | Disclosure |
|-------|------------|----------|------------|
| **PUBLIC** | Information that can be shared without restriction | Anyone | Website, marketing, PR |
| **CUSTOMER** | Information shared with paying customers | Customers under agreement | Documentation, training |
| **PARTNER** | Information shared with strategic partners | Partners under NDA | Integration docs, co-sell |
| **INTERNAL** | Information for employees only | Employees | Internal docs, wikis |
| **RESTRICTED** | Sensitive IP requiring need-to-know | Named individuals | Encrypted, access-logged |
| **NEVER** | Information that must not be disclosed | No one external | Core algorithms, keys |

### 2.2 Disclosure Violations

| Violation Type | Consequence |
|----------------|-------------|
| PUBLIC → disclosed correctly | None |
| CUSTOMER → disclosed to non-customer | Breach of trust, potential NDA violation |
| PARTNER → disclosed to non-partner | NDA violation, legal exposure |
| INTERNAL → disclosed externally | Disciplinary action, potential termination |
| RESTRICTED → unauthorized disclosure | Termination, legal action |
| NEVER → any disclosure | Termination, legal action, injunctive relief |

## 7. Disclosure Scenarios

### 7.1 Sales Conversations

**May Disclose:**
- All PUBLIC information
- CUSTOMER information after signing (or during trial with click-wrap)
- Demo data and sample visualizations
- General capability descriptions
- Competitive differentiation talking points

**Must Not Disclose:**
- Specific algorithmic details
- Exact threshold values
- Internal documentation
- Other customer data or examples

### 7.2 Marketing Content

**May Include:**
- PUBLIC information only
- Screenshots with sample/demo data
- Video demos with synthetic data
- Testimonials (with customer approval)
- General architecture diagrams (conceptual only)

**Must Not Include:**
- Formula details
- Coefficient values
- Real customer data
- Internal document excerpts

### 7.3 Customer Support

**May Disclose:**
- All PUBLIC and CUSTOMER information
- Troubleshooting guidance
- Feature documentation
- Their own data and metrics

**Must Not Disclose:**
- Other customer data
- Internal system details beyond documentation
- Algorithm specifics not in customer docs

### 7.4 Partner Discussions

**May Disclose (Under NDA):**
- PUBLIC, CUSTOMER, and PARTNER information
- Integration specifications
- Technical architecture (high-level)
- Roadmap (with appropriate caveats)

**Must Not Disclose:**
- INTERNAL, RESTRICTED, or NEVER information
- Detailed algorithms
- Other partner terms

### 7.5 Investor Communications

**May Disclose (Under NDA):**
- PUBLIC through INTERNAL information
- High-level technical differentiation
- Aggregate metrics and growth data
- Competitive positioning

**Should Limit:**
- RESTRICTED information (summarize, don't detail)
- Specific implementation details

**Must Not Disclose:**
- NEVER information
- Individual customer data

### 7.6 Press/Analyst Briefings

**May Disclose:**
- PUBLIC information only
- Approved talking points
- Authorized demos

**Must Not Disclose:**
- Anything beyond PUBLIC without explicit PR approval
- Speculative features
- Customer names without permission

## 9. Incident Response

### 9.1 Suspected Disclosure

If unauthorized disclosure is suspected:

1. **Immediate**: Notify legal and security
2. **Assess**: Determine classification level and scope
3. **Contain**: Revoke access, secure channels
4. **Investigate**: Determine cause and extent
5. **Remediate**: Address vulnerability
6. **Document**: Maintain incident record

### 9.2 Required Disclosure

In cases of required legal disclosure:

1. **Notify**: Alert legal immediately
2. **Minimize**: Disclose only what is legally required
3. **Protect**: Request protective order if possible
4. **Document**: Maintain record of compelled disclosure
